Watermelon Mint

Yield: 12 oz (355 ml)

Description

Cooling, crisp, and ultra-hydrating, Watermelon Mint is summer in a bottle. Fresh, seedless watermelon meets a kiss of lime and garden mint for a clean, revitalizing sip with no sugar added.

Serve ice-cold after a workout or as a sunny afternoon refresher. The delicate aromatics of mint lift the natural sweetness of melon, while a touch of citrus keeps the finish bright and lively.

Ingredients (for 12 oz)

  • 3 cups seedless watermelon, chilled, rind removed, cubed (about 450–500 g)
  • 1/4 small lime, peeled (or 1 tsp fresh lime juice) for brightness
  • 6–8 fresh mint leaves
  • Optional: 1/4 inch fresh ginger, peeled (gentle warmth)
  • Optional: pinch of fine sea salt (electrolyte boost)

Tip: Soft fruits yield more pulp—aim for ~13–14 oz pre-strain to finish at ~12 oz after filtering.

Equipment

  • Cold-press (masticating) juicer
  • Mylk bag or fine-mesh strainer (for extra-smooth texture)
  • BPA-free bottle or glass mason jar with lid

Steps to Make 12 oz

  1. Chill the watermelon for 1–2 hours. Remove rind and any black seeds. Peel the lime section; rinse mint.
  2. Cut melon into chute-friendly pieces.
  3. Power on the juicer. Feed watermelon in batches; finish with lime and mint (sandwich mint between melon for better extraction).
  4. (Optional) Strain through a mylk bag or fine-mesh strainer for a polished, low-pulp finish.
  5. Bottle immediately. Chill 30–60 minutes for best flavor.
  6. Store sealed in the refrigerator and enjoy within 3–5 days. Shake gently before serving.

Notes & Adjustments

  • More mint-forward? Add 2–3 extra leaves or lightly bruise mint before juicing.
  • Brighter/tarter? Increase lime slightly.
  • Less sweet / extra-hydrating? Add 1/4 small cucumber.
  • Frothy mocktail: serve over ice with a splash of sparkling water.
